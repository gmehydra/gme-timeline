DRS
Direct Registration System
Summary
  • Cede & Co., a private company and partner of the DTC, legally owns the large majority of all issued stocks in the United States
    • As of 1998, Cede & Co. owned 83% of all issued stocks in the United States. Since 1998, this ownership percentage has not been publicly released again
    • Any time shares of a company are purchased at a stock broker, the legal owner of those shares is Cede & Co.
    • Due to lack of transparency by Cede & Co. and the DTC, the number of share entitlements that exist among stock brokers and other market participants cannot be verified
  • DRS is an acronym for Direct Registration System
    • DRS is a mechanism that enables investors to remove shares from the DTC's ownership and registers them with the issuing company's transfer agent in the name of the investor, making them them the legal owner of the shares
    • Holding shares in DRS form is the only way to have genuine legal ownership of your shares
  • GameStop's transfer agent is Computershare
  • GME shareholders are challenging the status quo by directly registering billions of dollars worth of shares, owning approximately 14% of GameStop in DRS holdings. No other company in history has had a large portion of their shares owned by directly registered shareholders
Held by registered holders at Computershare: DRS versus DSPP

All shares held with GameStop's transfer agent Computershare are considered "registered", which means that the individual investor is listed by name on the issuer's (GameStop's) stock ledger (also referred to as the "register") and is thus known to the company.
However, of registered shares, there is a significant distinction between shares held in DRS form versus shares held in DSPP form.

  • DRS
    • Shares are legally owned and held by the individual shareholder without any intermediary
    • Though the shares are accessed and managed in an account with Computershare, the shares are "held" directly by the shareholder
    • Directly registered in the shareholder's name on GameStop's stock ledger (or register), in the main class
  • DSPP
    • The direct stock purchase plan (DSPP) is a proprietary Computershare service
    • Shares held in DSPP are not legally owned and held by the investor
      • 80% to 90% of the shares are legally owned and held by Dingo & Co (Computershare's nominee)
      • 10% to 20% of the shares are legally owned and held by Cede & Co for operational efficiency
    • The investor is a beneficial owner of these shares and not the legal owner
    • The investor is registered as the (beneficial) owner of the shares in a subclass on the ledger

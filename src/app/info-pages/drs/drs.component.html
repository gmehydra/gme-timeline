DRS
Direct Registration System
Summary
  • Cede & Co., a private company and partner of the DTC, owns the large majority of all issued stocks in the United States
    • As of 1998, Cede & Co. owned 83% of all issued stocks in the United States. Since 1998, this ownership percentage has not been publicly released again
    • Any time shares of a company are purchased at a stock broker, the legal owner of those shares is Cede & Co.
    • Due to lack of transparency by Cede & Co. and the DTC, the number of share entitlements that exist among stock brokers and other market participants cannot be verified
  • DRS is an acronym for Direct Registration System
    • DRS is the only way to obtain sole legal title ownership of your shares
    • DRS is a mechanism that enables shareholders to remove shares from the DTC's ownership and registers them with the issuing company's transfer agent
  • GameStop's transfer agent is Computershare
  • GME shareholders are challenging the status quo by directly registering billions of dollars worth of shares, owning approximately 21% of GameStop. No other company in history has had a large portion of their shares owned by directly registered shareholders
Held by registered holders at Computershare: DRS versus DSPP

All shares held with GameStop's transfer agent Computershare are considered "registered", but of those shares there is a distinction between DRS shares and DSPP shares.

  • DRS
    • Registered in the name of the holder
    • Held in book-entry form
    • Held by record holders at Computershare
    • Holding with DRS provides both title ownership and possession of shares
  • DSPP
    • Registered in the name of the holder
    • Held in book-entry form
    • Held by Dingo & Co (a nominee for Computershare)
    • Holding with DSPP provides title ownership but not possession of shares
